In the world of indie gaming, few titles capture the essence of emotional storytelling and engaging gameplay like "Save My Scrap." Set to launch on April 22, 2026, on Steam, this visual novel and puzzle game invites players into a touching narrative where they repair a broken android named Harima. The game not only focuses on mechanical fixes but also explores the deep connections formed between characters, making it a unique experience that resonates with players on multiple levels.

Originally based on the popular free game "Fix My Junk," developed by Unio Anou, "Save My Scrap" promises a definitive edition with enhanced features, including new dialogues, endings, and a gallery mode. The heart-wrenching story, combined with beautiful cyberpunk art, ensures that players will not just be repairing an android but also uncovering long-buried memories and emotional truths.

As we anticipate the release of this game, it's exciting to think about how indie developers like AMATA Games are pushing the boundaries of storytelling in video games. Will "Save My Scrap" redefine how we perceive relationships in gaming?
